# Is the gate-vs-descriptor discordance INTERMITTENCY or MORPHOLOGY?

The two-stage audit leaves ~41% of gate-flagged generalized recordings with no band-power corroboration. Because the gate re-run kept Morgoth's per-segment probability (guard disabled), we can split that residual by his OWN view of how much of the recording is slow — the fraction of segments with p_gen_30 > 0.5.

Among **11,312** gated-generalized recordings, **4,298 (38.0%)** are discordant. Morgoth's own per-segment prevalence is markedly lower in those (median **0.28** vs **0.48** in the corroborated ones): the descriptor tends to fall silent exactly where Morgoth's signal is sparse.

| the discordance is… | Morgoth's per-segment prevalence | n | share of discordant |
|---|---|---|---|
| intermittency (very sparse) | < 10% of segments | 889 | 20.7% |
| intermittency | 10–50% of segments | 2,314 | 53.8% |
| **morphology gap** | **≥ 50% of segments** | **1,095** | **25.5%** |

So **~75% of the apparent discordance is intermittency** — sparse slowing that a band-power rule needing a minimum share of abnormal segments cannot help but out-vote. The genuine **morphology gap** — Morgoth generalized-slow in ≥50% of segments yet band power flat — is only **1,095 recordings = 9.7% of gated-generalized, 4.3% of all analysed**. The headline ~40% is mostly a threshold artefact of intermittency, not a model-vs-descriptor chasm; the irreducible morphology miss is a few percent.

Corroboration rises monotonically with Morgoth's per-segment prevalence (35% at <10% of segments → 75% at >75%), the same internal-consistency signature seen against his recording-level confidence.
