# Coverage by sleep stage × age — do we need more data?

Counts = # recordings with ≥3 usable segments in that stage (so the per-recording per-stage value is
stable). Generated by the coverage cell in analysis; regenerate after general staging completes.

## Normal group (the reference — what the curves are built from)
```
band     W    N1   N2   N3   REM
0-2    218   187  233  154    62
3-5    135    80  102   26    52
6-12   220    98  141   32   122
13-17  227    73  108   13   127
18-29  723   366  283   42   341
30-44  627   377  211   25   245
45-59  765   473  292   27   231
60-74  763   447  246   16   192
75+    384   244  143   15    69
```

**Read-out**
- **W, N1, N2 — well powered across the whole lifespan** (≥73/band, mostly hundreds). Curves for
  these are trustworthy including tails.
- **N3 — under-powered from age ≥3, and it thins further with age** (154 at 0–2, then 13–42/band).
  This is *partly physiological* (N3 declines with age; elderly have little) and *partly sampling*
  (routine ~10-min clips rarely capture deep sleep). → N3 curves above ~childhood have wide CIs; our
  fitter already truncates N3 ~age 34 (min effective-n guard). This gap may be **unfixable with
  routine EEG**; the fix if needed is longer/overnight recordings (PSG) for N3, esp. in adults/elderly.
- **REM — adequate in adults, thin at the extremes** (0–2: 62, 75+: 69). Borderline; widen the age
  kernel there.

## Pathological group (focal shown; general still staging)
```
focal_slow  W   N1  N2  N3  REM     (single digits in most cells)
0-2         1    1   2   2   1
...        (mostly 1–20/cell; N3 often 0–5)
```
**Read-out:** far too few abnormal cases **per stage × age band** for stage-specific *validation*
(often 0–5/cell). Generalized (~5,396) will help but, split over 45 cells, many stay thin.

## Recommendations
1. **Normal norms:** W/N1/N2 are sufficient — ship them. For **N3 (age ≥3) and REM extremes**, either
   (a) accept wider CIs + a hard **min-n gate** (don't emit a stage×age norm below ~n=30; fall back to
   a coarser age-pooled norm), and/or (b) **source deep sleep from longer/overnight recordings** if
   N3 norms in adults are clinically needed. Report `n_eff` alongside every curve.
2. **Pathological validation:** stage-specific discrimination is **currently underpowered** — validate
   discrimination **stage-pooled** (well powered: 2,067 focal / 5,396 generalized) and treat
   per-stage abnormal analysis as **exploratory** until more staged abnormal cases are collected.
3. **If collecting more:** prioritize (i) **abnormal (focal & generalized) recordings that include
   sleep**, and (ii) **normal deep sleep (N3) across adult ages** — those are the two real gaps.
   W/N1/N2 normal and stage-pooled abnormal do **not** need more.
4. **Practical guardrail in code:** the curve fitter already NaNs cells below a min effective-n; add
   the same gate to scoring so we never score a patient's stage against a thin/absent norm (fall back
   to the stage-pooled norm and say so).
